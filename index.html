Tribute to Dr. Norman Borlaug

Dr. Norman Borlaug was an American agronomist who led initiatives worldwide that contributed to the extensive increases in agricultural production known as the Green Revolution. Born on March 25, 1914, he received the Nobel Peace Prize for his work, which is credited with saving over a billion people from starvation.

Dr. Borlaug's work led to the development of high-yield, disease-resistant crops, which were instrumental in fighting hunger in developing countries.
